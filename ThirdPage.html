Climbing Route Difficulty

Climbing Routes are almost always given a difficulty grade. These grades are meant to represent how difficult it is to complete the given route. The assigned grade is a subjective decision made by the person who set the route, or the person who climbed it first. This means that you will not always agree with the chosen grade. This is fine since they are meant as a guideline to help climbers make decisions on which routes to climb.

There are many different grading schemes used around the world. The most commonly used in North America is the YDS (Yosemite Decimal System). In this system a grade of 5.0 would indicate a climb similar to a steep ladder, while a grade of 5.15 would represent an overhanging cliff.
