Equipment Needed to Climb

  • Climbing Shoes: These shoes are much stiffer than traditional shoes and typically fit much smaller. This combination of features means that wearing them mamkes it much easier to place your feet on small foot holds.
  • Chalk Bag: Chalk is used to keep your hands dry to prevent them from slipping when climbing for long periods of time. Chalk Bages allow you to bring your chalk with you on the wall by clipping it to your harness.
  • Harness: Climbing harnesses are used to attach yourself to the rope if you are climbing or attach yourself to the belay device if you are the one belaying.
  • Belay Device: Belay devices are used to remove the slack from the clibmer's rope and keep them safe. There are two types of belay devices, GriGris and ATCs.
    • GriGri: A GriGri is the more expensive of the two belay devices, however they have many more safety mechanisms if something were to go wrong.
    • ATC: An ATC is the less expensive option compared to a GriGri, they have a much simpler design however this comes at the cost of losing safety mechanisms.

Outdoor only gear

  • Climbing Grade Rope: Rope is needed to keep the climber safe, climbing rope is stretchy and durable allowing for repeated use without breaking. A good rope will mean that even long fall will not be too uncomfortable. Indoor climbing gyms will already have ropes in place meaning that you only need your own rope if you intend to climb outdoors.
  • Quickdraws: Quickdraws are needed for lead climbing to attach your rope to the pre-paced anchor points. Some climbing gyms will already have quickdraws or similar equipment in place, thus they are mostly needed for outdoor climbing.
  • Helmet: When climbing outdoors a helmet is often necessary since loose rocks above you may fall, causing serious damage if unprotected.
  • Cams: Cams are used in trad climbing as your anchor points. They can be easily be placed in cracks on the wall to offer support for your safety rope.
